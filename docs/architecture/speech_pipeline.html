5 · Key design decisions & open risks

Doctor-in-the-loop Every note is labelled "Ready for review." The doctor edits and finalises. They are the medico-legal author — the AI is a drafting assistant.
raw_transcript preserved Original ASR output stored in raw_transcript and never overwritten. Regeneration re-runs the interpreter so prompt fixes take effect retroactively.
Two-LLM handoff risk Step 2 English is ground truth for the SOAP generator. A wrong sentence in Step 2 (wrong pain score, wrong drug name) propagates undetected. Doctor review of the transcript tab is the only current safeguard.
nou / now ambiguity Prompt rules added but LLM still occasionally misreads "nou" (correction) as "now" (temporal) between two numbers. Pre-processing regex may be needed as a harder guard. See PATOIS_ASR_RESEARCH.md Q9.
Numerical value safety Pain scores, BP readings, medication doses, and gestational age are highest-risk fields. Any numeral misread or inversion is a clinical error. No automated range-check exists yet.
Old session backfill Sessions created before the raw_transcript field get their raw tokens reconstructed on-the-fly from Step 1 left-side tokens via _reconstruct_raw_from_step1(). Accurate for most cases.
Prompt size PATOIS_INTERPRETER_SYSTEM_PROMPT ≈ 4 200 tokens. JAMAICAN_CONTEXT_ADDENDUM ≈ 750 tokens. Both are within acceptable limits for the task complexity.
MMS bias MMS has no Patois-specific training data. Output is phonetic English approximation of Patwa speech. Systematic biases expected for tonal emphasis, vowel clusters, and unstressed syllables. See research brief.
